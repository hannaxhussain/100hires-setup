## Post 3

**Date: 23/06/26**

**Post Link: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADZugZQBAOYga9I_gPtLzixBUuymCOlUo1Q](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADZugZQBAOYga9I_gPtLzixBUuymCOlUo1Q)**

**Topic:** Two brands can run identical on-page optimization and get cited by AI at completely different rates.

**Summary:** on-page optimization is increasingly becoming something that can and should be largely automated. The bigger differentiator is maintaining clear, consistent messaging across every touchpoint. That's what ultimately influences how LLMs understand and reference a brand. On-page and technical aren't a competing lever, they're the floor you can still fall through. If a model can't reliably crawl or parse you, or your branded SERP contradicts what's said elsewhere, the external trust has nothing to attach to.

**Key Takeaways:**

- Takeaway 1 on- page SEO is table stakes, not a differentiator

- Takeaway 2 consistent messages across touchpoints is the real differentiator

- Takeaway 3 broken trust signals undermine everything else
