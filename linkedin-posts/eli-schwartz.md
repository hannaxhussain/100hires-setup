**Topic:** Context Window = AI's Working Memory

**Summary:**
The post explains the concept of an AI model's **context window**, which is the maximum amount of text or information it can process at one time. As conversations become longer, older information may be dropped, causing the AI to appear as though it has "forgotten" earlier instructions. It also notes that larger context windows do not always improve performance, as models can struggle to retrieve relevant details from very long inputs. The post highlights the "Lost in the Middle" effect, where information placed in the middle of long prompts is more likely to be overlooked than content at the beginning or end.


**Key Takeaways:**
- Takeaway 1 AI has memory limits
- Takeaway 2 More context isn't always better
- Takeaway 3 Prompt structure matters
